Library of Unfinished Books

In the quiet corners of my apartment, a tangible weight persists - a stack of unfinished books, each spine a silent witness to my intentions and the unmet promises to myself. This literary graveyard serves as a poignant reminder of narratives left suspended, knowledge left undigested, and the daunting task of forgiving myself, especially in the relentless embrace of hustle culture.

These books became casualties.
Neglected in the ceaseless hustle to meet deadlines and exceed expectations.
In the race to achieve more, literary exploration took a backseat, and my books became artifacts of unfulfilled aspirations.

Forgiveness, in this context, becomes an act of rebellion against the unforgiving nature of hustle culture. It's a recognition that self-worth is not solely tied to productivity metrics and completed to-do lists. The acknowledgment of life's complex demands and the need for balance becomes paramount. Embracing forgiveness is reclaiming agency over my time, acknowledging that the worth of a person is not solely measured by the speed at which they move but by the depth of their experiences.

As I extend forgiveness to myself, I also extend grace to the unfinished stories on my shelf. They await my return, not as burdens but as patient companions in the journey of self-discovery. In the quiet rebellion against the pressures of hustle culture, I rediscover the joy of exploration and the richness of a life lived beyond the confines of perpetual motion.
